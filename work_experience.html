Work Experience

Machine Learning Engineer at Huawei Research Centre, IC-Lab, Edmonton, Canada

Winter 2020- Now

  • Our focus for the last two years has been developing novel neural architecture search (NAS) algorithms, in the AutoML pipeline. Hand-crafting deep neural architectures requires a vast amount of effort and task-specific, human-expert knowledge. On the other hand, the automation of neural architecture search also involves searching a giant search space and performing expensive evaluations. In the NAS team, we are re-thinking how to search smarter and more efficiently. In other words, our initial goal was to improve any user-defined network in terms of different optimization objectives. We even have set our objectives higher by improving the top architectures in well-known NAS benchmark datasets such as NAS-Bench 101, 201, 301 and many other internal networks that directly affect the cutting-edge technology used in Huawei products. Our novelty is detecting useful sub-graph structures and rank them based on our scoring system. Then we incrementally improve the input network by adapting Bayesian/RL-Based methods for optimization.
  • A major portion of my job is the continuous transfer of knowledge from the data and results. I am responsible for the illustration of data and reporting to different teams
