Academic Background

Useful Policy Invariant Shaping from Arbitrary Advice

Master's Thesis from the University of Alberta, Department of Computing Science, Edmonton, Canada

September 2017- February 2020
The learning of a reinforcement learning (RL) agent can be greatly accelerated by incorporating external/expert advice. The key challenge is to use external advice without altering the optimal policy of the agent. Potential-based reward shaping (PBRS) holds promise but is limited by the need for a well-defined potential function. This thesis focuses on admitting arbitrary advice and improving performance without affecting the optimal policy. The main contribution of this work is to expose, theoretically and empirically, a flaw in the well-stablished dynamic potential based advice (DPBA) method and further to introduce an alternative method called policy invariant explicit shaping (PIES). This work shows theoretically and empirically that PIES is a simple alternative that succeeds where DPBA fails.
